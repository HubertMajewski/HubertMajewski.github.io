New York City Teens Connection

Journey of preventing teenage pregnancy across the north shore of Staten Island.

NYC DOHMH Youth Leadership Team

New York City Teens Connection (NYCTC) is a program of the New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (NYCDOHMH) that brings different groups of people together (young adults, parents, health centers, schools, community based organizations, and citywide agencies) to help reduce unintended teen pregnancy in New York City with a focus on the Bronx, Central Brooklyn and the North Shore of Staten Island.

NYCTC partners with local high schools and youth organizations to implement evidence-based sexual health programs for young adults and then links those schools and organizations to community clinics that provide high quality health services to teens. NYCTC also brings community members and youth serving organizations together to help raise awareness about young adults’ rights to access sexual and reproductive health education, clinical services, like obtaining contraception, and services that empower teens to make healthy decisions.

The Staten Island Youth Leadership Team (YLT) is made up of 15-19 year old teens living and/or going to school in the North Shore of Staten Island, who want to help reduce the high rates of unintended teen pregnancy. The YLT works to establish healthy communities where all teens are supported, and have access to comprehensive health services and education. The YLT’s Youth Leaders participate in annual conferences, community events, peer health education opportunities, and increase awareness of unintended teen pregnancy and teen sexual and reproductive health rights in their schools and communities. YLT members receive leadership training, gain valuable knowledge of adolescent sexual and reproductive health topics, and help inform the creation of local and citywide projects to help promote healthy sexual and reproductive outcomes for teens.
